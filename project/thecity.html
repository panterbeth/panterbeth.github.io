History of Lake Stevens

Lake Stevens was settled in 1886 on a homestead that was 160 acres along the eastern shore. The first town "Ferry" was established in 1890 and later became "Hartford". in 1905 the Rucker Brothers Timber Company linked Hardford and Lake Stevens by building a railroad spur. In 1919 Rucker Mill was burned and partially rebuilt. In 1925 it burned a second time but instead of rebuilding it was dismantled. Lake Stevens was a resort community during the 1920s to the 1950s. In 1960 with a population of 900 Lake Stevens was incorporated as a City. In 2008 the city had a population of 14,554. The lake, which covers approximately 1,000 acres is the recreational focal point of the Lake Stevens community.
